# thai-auto-dashboard
Thailand has long been regarded as the "Detroit of Asia," serving as one of Southeast Asia's most significant automotive manufacturing and consumption hubs. Over the past five years, however, the industry has entered a period of profound structural transformation — one that extends far beyond typical market cycles.
This project examines the Thai automotive market between 2020 and 2024, a period marked by the dual pressures of post-pandemic economic recovery and the accelerating global shift toward electric mobility. While traditional internal combustion engine (ICE) vehicles continue to dominate registration volumes, the emergence of EV brands — particularly Chinese manufacturers such as BYD, GWM, and NETA — has fundamentally altered the competitive landscape.
The analysis draws on vehicle registration data from the Department of Land Transport (DLT), macroeconomic indicators from the National Economic and Social Development Council (NESDC), and infrastructure data sourced from OpenStreetMap. Using a combination of descriptive statistics, OLS regression, and machine learning techniques, this project investigates the key economic and structural drivers behind both general market trends and EV adoption.
Key questions explored include: What macroeconomic factors most strongly influence total automotive demand? Why is EV growth driven by infrastructure and policy rather than fuel cost savings? And which brands are most sensitive to price and income changes in the Thai market?
The findings reveal a market in structural transition — where legacy brands hold volume but growth momentum has shifted decisively toward electrification, with significant implications for manufacturers, policymakers, and consumers alike.
